MALAPPURAM

Malappuram is a northern district in Kerala known for its rich culture, historic significance and remarkable heritage. The region of Malappuram, speckled with small hills and mounds, justifies its name which in Malayalam means ‘Hill Top’. With a history that weaves ancient and modern times together, the region contributed extensively to the culture, religion, economy as well as tourism of Kerala.

Top Destinations
PADINHAREKARA BEACH

Beaches have always been a scenic place where every tourist spend their moments with their loved ones. Padinharekara beach is one of the tourist spots that people want to visit most frequently. You can have a mesmerizing view of two rivers Bharathapuzha and Tirupuzha merging with Arabian sea. It is one of the best places to visit in Malappuram

KADALUNDI BIRD SANCTUARY

A remarkable sanctuary hosting more than 100s of native bird species and 60 migratory bird species is widely known for its location. The peculiarity of the place is that the river kadalundi puzha flows into the arabian sea . travellers who are keen to know about such places, Kadalundi bird sanctuary is the best place for them. This is one of the most beautiful places in Malappuram.

BIYYAM KAYAL

You would be surprised to know that Malappuram is famous for its stunning backwater lakes and the most stunning amongst them is Biyyam Kayal. It is located in proximity to Ponnani and has been entitled as the adventure zone in the entire area. If you are an adventure junkie and looking for some daring expeditions here then you must surely head to Biyyam Kayal on your vacation to Malappuram.

BHARATHAPUZHA RIVER

Also known as River Nilsa that provides shelter to 11 reservoirs with Malampuzha dam, it is the second longest river in Kerala. The river has been an integral part of the Malabar district and a sacred river for Hindus as there are mentions of the river in the ancient scriptures. This is one of the few rivers in Kerala with a spiritual and cultural significance for people, especially the famous cultural house of the state Kalamandalam is situated on the banks of the river.

TEAK MUSEUM

The first teak museum, it was established in 1994 and is located 4 km from Nilambur. The museum has two floors that feature an extensive collection of articles and documents depicting the artistic value of wood and its history. You’ll find many teak forests in Kerala as it occurs naturally in southern India.
